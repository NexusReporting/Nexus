Program Reference · Accenture Song / AWS
Glossary — Terms, Acronyms, and Systems
All key terms, acronyms, and systems referenced across the Nexus demand platform
Program and pipeline terms
Nexus Program
The Accenture Song demand generation program built for AWS. Its goal is to generate and qualify VMware-to-AWS migration leads from Sub-Large Enterprise companies (fewer than 2,000 VMs) and hand them to the AWS sales team via the ACE pipeline. All infrastructure is Accenture-owned until the ACE handoff.
MEL Stage
Marketing Eligible Lead. A contact who has cleared the disqualification gate, has marketing consent, and has been classified as eligible for nurture or calling. MEL is a lifecycle stage in Eloqua, not a quality tier — it means the contact is in-program and routing has been assigned. Used for contacts with marketing consent who have not yet qualified for sales engagement.
MQP Stage
Marketing Qualified Pipeline. A MEL who has VMware confirmed, sales + AI consent, a phone number, and a timezone/state. MQPs are routed to NLPearl for AI outbound calling. Partial qualification data that does not meet threshold does not advance to MQP — the stage gates who gets called, not who is ultimately qualified.
PO Stage
Partner Opportunity. A contact who has passed the ACE Readiness Gate: 100+ VMs, migration timeline under 18 months, and full sales + AI consent. POs are the deliverable handed to the AWS sales team via the ACE pipeline. Target for the full program is 900 POs over 4 quarters.
ACE System
AWS Customer Engagement. The partner portal where Accenture registers qualified migration opportunities for the AWS sales team. ACE upload is currently a manual process — REST API automation is the target state. First ACE upload is being prepared as of May 26 by Connor, Ian, and Paulo. 0 POs uploaded to date.
AIP+ System
Accenture Insights Platform Plus. Cloud-based analytics-as-a-service that serves as the cross-system signal unification layer for Nexus. AIP+ is the target destination for the ECID-to-Eloqua contact ID join, which is the #1 measurement infrastructure priority post-MVP.
NLPearl System
AI-powered outbound voice agent that calls MQP-stage contacts. NLPearl conducts structured qualification conversations, captures VM count, migration timeline, and consent confirmation. Call results are written back to Eloqua as field updates that trigger MEL or PO routing.
ECID ID
Experience Cloud ID. Adobe's cross-device persistent identifier assigned by Adobe Analytics when a visitor lands on an AEM page. The ECID-to-Eloqua contact ID join is the critical missing link that would allow behavioral web data to be attributed to known contacts in Eloqua. Not yet implemented.
MAP Program
Migration Acceleration Program. AWS's partner incentive program that provides funding and resources for customers migrating workloads to AWS. Nexus leads who qualify as POs may be eligible for MAP benefits, which the AWS sales team and migration partners manage after the ACE handoff.
CTOR Metric
Click-to-Open Rate. The percentage of email recipients who opened the email and then clicked a link. Calculated as clicks divided by unique opens. CTOR is a more reliable engagement signal than open rate alone because it filters out privacy-enabled auto-opens and measures intent among those who actually viewed the content.
Sub-Large Enterprise Segment
Nexus's target customer segment: companies running fewer than 2,000 VMware VMs. These accounts are too large for the standard self-service AWS migration path but too small for the enterprise white-glove engagement model. The ~$20B TAM comes from this segment.
HQL Lead Type
Human-Qualified Lead. A lead type on the TechTarget / NetLine content syndication platform where a vendor representative has confirmed the lead's accuracy and relevance via a direct conversation before delivery. All 95 NetLine leads from the May 7–12 batch were HQL at lead score 4.
TechTarget Vendor
A B2B content syndication publisher similar to NetLine, used to distribute Nexus content assets to targeted IT audiences. Generates inbound leads with pre-captured marketing consent.
Adobe Launch System
The tag management layer used to deploy and manage Adobe Analytics tracking on AEM pages. Responsible for firing page view and event tracking including form interactions, clicks, and UTM capture. Adobe Launch intercepts UTM parameters on page load and persists them as cookies, making them available as dimensions within Adobe Analytics for attribution reporting. Required for enabling behavioral data collection feeding Adobe Analytics. Tagging work is owned by Allison Melendez's team.
Unknown Visitor Data Data Type
Anonymous behavioral data captured by Adobe Analytics for visitors without an identified contact record (no Eloqua ID). Includes page views, click events, session activity, and UTM-based attribution tied to ECID. Once a visitor submits a form, this unknown history becomes joinable to a known Eloqua contact record, enabling partial reconstruction of pre-conversion behavior. For Nexus MVP, ingestion of this anonymous behavioral data into AIP+ is dependent on Adobe Analytics tagging and integration enablement, which is currently not fully active.
Data Reconciliation / Integrity Controls Process
Processes that validate consistency across CS batch files, Eloqua contact creation (upsert_contact), NLPearl call results, and ACE submissions. Primary control: row-count reconciliation between intake and system-of-record objects.
Key metrics
MET001 — Funnel Volume Metric
Total count of contacts actively in the Nexus pipeline at any given time. Includes all lifecycle stages from Known through PO minus DQ'd records. As of May 26: 264 (169 Eloqua in-flight + 95 new NetLine leads pending intake).
MET056 — Pickup Rate Metric
Percentage of NLPearl call attempts where the contact answered the phone. Calculated as connected calls divided by total attempts. Peak week (Apr 27): 24% (44 of 183). The B2B average is 3–10%, making Nexus's rate a strong positive signal.
MET062 — Speed-to-Call SLA Metric
The percentage of MQP-stage contacts for whom NLPearl initiated a call attempt within 24 hours of the contact reaching MQP status. Currently 100% compliant. Industry research shows leads contacted within 1 hour convert at 53% vs 17% at 24h — tightening this SLA is the next leverage point.
MET054 — Call Signal Metric
The outcome tag applied by NLPearl after a connected call. Four categories: Strong (PO candidate — 100+ VMs, under 18mo timeline, consent), Weak (monitor / re-attempt), Negative (recycle), No Connect. As of Apr 27 week: 17 Strong, 20 Weak, 0 Negative, 6 No Connect.
